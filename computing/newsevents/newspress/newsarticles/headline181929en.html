TU858 Student wins Prestigious Undergraduate Award

 

Jennifer Nolan a TU858 Computer Science (International) Graduate has been named as the ‘Regional Winner’ in the Computer Science category for the Undergraduate Awards. The prestigious award was bestowed on Jennifer for her final year project as part of TU858 Computer Science (International) degree.

Jennifer was part of the third cohort of students to matriculate the DT282 course. The course itself was established in 2014 and was designed as a four-year honour's degree in core computing & programming skills with a key international and globalisation focus. The course was a first of its kind in Ireland and unique in that it offered its graduates highly sought after IT industry skills coupled with a foreign language and an understanding of the Global IT Industry

Jennifer consistently excelled during her time in DT282/TU858 being awarded ‘Best in Year’ at the DIT Computer Science Awards in both her first and second year. Earlier this year Jennifer was awarded the Luan O’Carroll Memorial Award at the TU Dublin Computer Science Project Fair.

Jennifer will now go on to compete in the Computer Science category at the Undergraduate Awards Global Summit which will be hosted virtually as an online event for the first time on November 16th -18th 2020. Jennifer joins a long list of DIT School of Computing graduates to be commended and/or to have won the Overall Global Awards, but she is the first to do so as a TU Dublin student.

The Undergraduate awards are cited as the ultimate champion for high-potential undergraduates. It is the world’s largest academic awards programme so by making this list, these students are can be said to be in the top 10% in their respective fields worldwide.

TU Dublin Computer Science September 2020.
